Visualising the text of Red Riding Hood

The data for this visualisation is extracted from the text of the picture book, Red Riding Hood, written and illustrated by Lydia Louisa Anna Very. This book is thought to be one of the first shape books ever printed in America, published by Prang in 1863.

The "How to Read" section will explain the various ways to read and navigate the visualisation. To find out more on this project, refer to the About page.

Note: There may be limitations to the functionality and accessibility of this page. It works best on a non-touch screen device, Chrome or Firefox browser.

Structure

The visualisation is divided into 2 main parts:

  • Overview
  • Page
    • Visual
    • Text

Overview

On the left is a sentiment scale. The highest point (dark gold) is most positive and the lowest (dark grey) most negative. The middle (white) is neutral and also depicted with a horizontal light grey dashed line to serve as a baseline. A page is represented with a circle starting from 1 at the left, ending at 16 on the right. Each page is plotted vertically based on its sentiment value; so if it's higher more positive, lower more negative. Also the circle is coloured based on sentiment value. The number of words per page is also represented in a subtle way with a thin line around the circle. Starting at the top, is the first word moving clockwise around. The 49 words forms a full circle, so the more full the line around the circle, the more words the page has.

Page - Visual

The visual section can be read in sections. The inner circle has a summary of the page. Moving outwards is the emotions section which there can be up to six on a page depicted by triangles. From top moving clockwise, joy, fear, surprise, anger.... The larger the triangle, larger the emotion value. The next section is sentences of which there are always 8 in a page represented by thin curved lines. The first sentence start at the top moving clockwise. Each sentence starts black, fading into light gray at the end of a sentence and is linked with words which is the final outer section. The first word starts at the top and moves clockwise around the circle. Also like each page, a word can have a postive or negative sentiment thus coloured and shaded accordingly to its value. Each page also has 2 sets of rhyming words, the first set will have a single black side line, and the second set double black side lines.

Some additional information can be seen by hovering over certain elements. The inner circle will show the value of the page sentiment and top emotion. Each emotion triangle will show its type and value. Each word that has a positive of negative sentiment will show its value. If the word is neutral, it will be white and not have a value.

Page Text

The text section is very simple and straight forward displayed close to it's original format of 8 lines of text, for readibility and comparison. Upon hover, it hides the text only showing the punctuation of the page.

Navigation

Scrolling down shows the pages sequence. The circles in the overview also acts as navigation menu and enables jumping from page to page.
