Visualising the text of Red Riding Hood

The data for this visualisation is extracted from the text of the picture book, Red Riding Hood, written and illustrated by Lydia Louisa Anna Very. This book is thought to be one of the first shape books ever printed in America, published by Prang in 1863.

The aim of this project was to extract data from the text of the story, and visualising it in an experimental way to provide a companion to reading the story in it's original text.

To find out more on this project, refer to the About page.

Note: There may be limitations to the functionality and accessibility of this page. It works best on a non-touch screen device, Chrome or Firefox browser.

Introducing the Characters in the book

The section above lists all the characters starting with ones with the most mentions at the top to the least at the bottom. There are 5 characters in the story which are the ones above the line and the author also addresses 3 more that aren't in the story. The Reader, Reader's Mother and Reader's Wolf below the line.

On the left, symbols are given to each character. Red Riding Hood is given a fully filled red circle, her mother slightly filled, and her grandmother the least filled. The Wolf is given a black triangle, and when in disguise, a inside the same circle as Grandma. The Reader, Reader's Mother and Reader's Wolf, have the same symbols as Red Riding Hood, Mother, and Wolf but in grey.

On the right of the character names, are horizontal sections and their lengths correspond to the number to mentions on the far right. Hovering over any of the characters will bring up a tooltip with a bit more information about the character.

The Locations

There are 4 main locations. These are the places and environments where Red Riding Hood's journey takes her in the story, starting from her own home in the woods, through the forest to her grandmother's home. Like with characters, there is a symbol for each location, but the number of counts the occurence on a page, depicted on a horizontal bar chart.

Pages

There are 16 pages in this book. Each page has 8 lines and 2 sets of rhyming words. Each page is represented by a circle, with page 1 starting on the left moving right with each page increment. The vertical position and colour of each circle is based on its page sentiment value. The higher and golden, the more positive the sentiment. The lower and greyer, the more negative. The thin outer line surrounding each circle is the number of words per page. The longer the line, the more words on that page. Hovering over each circle will show the numerical value of sentiment value and word count of each page.

Individual pages

In this section, we go deeper looking into each page in more detail looking at more data. There are quite a lot to it so some reading the section below might be needed to understand the details.

These sections are divided up and numbered sections, for ease of explanation, but can be viewed in any order.

This is the original text as it was written in the book. Upon hover, the text disappears leaving only the punctuation.

The inner smallest circle shows a summary of the page. The default view shows the page number, number of words, and rhyming words. On hover show the sentiment value and the top emotion.

Moving outwards is the emotions section. There can be up to six on a page depicted by triangles. From top moving clockwise, joy, surprise, sadness, fear, disgust, anger. The larger the triangle, larger the emotion value.

The next section is sentences of which there are always 8 on a page represented by thin curved lines. The first sentence start at the top moving clockwise. Each sentence starts black, fading into light gray at the end of a sentence and is linked with words which is the final outer section.

The first word starts at the top and moves clockwise around the circle. Also like each page, a word can have a postive or negative sentiment thus coloured and shaded accordingly to its value. Each page also has 2 sets of rhyming words, the first set will have a single black side line, and the second set double black side lines.
